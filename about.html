Banaue Rice Terraces facts conjure an impressive image and one that illustrates a high level of structural knowledge. The awe-inspiring Banaue terraces are said to be one of the most impressive feats in engineering in the world. Upon visiting, that is not an understatement. The terraces were carved into the mountains more than 2000 years ago, and today, it is many members of the very same families who created the terraces that mind them. As most can imagine, erosion and other environmental factors have posed many threats to this amazing Banaue attractions so in the wake of these realizations, Banaue became protected as a National Cultural Treasure and has been a UNESCO World Heritage since 1995.

Rice production began upon completion of the terraces by Ifagao tribes, original Taiwanese migrants. For centuries they have successfully remained molded into the mountains and remained in harmony between humans and nature.
